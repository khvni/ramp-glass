# Seb-Style 10-Minute Demo Script

- `0:00-0:20` “We built every employee at Keysight their own AI coworker.”

- `0:20-0:35` “The models are good enough. The harness isn’t.”

- `0:35-1:05` “At Keysight, only about 2,000 of 20,000 employees are on Claude Code, Codex, or GitHub Copilot. That means most people are still stuck. Not because they lack ambition. Not because models are not good enough. Because they do not have an environment where AI can actually do real work.”

- `1:05-1:30` “Company context is scattered. Tools are disconnected. Setup is still too technical. We created AI urgency without enough infrastructure for rest of company to benefit.”

- `1:30-1:55` “So we built Tinker: an AI coworker for rest of Keysight. Goal is not to lower ceiling for experts. Goal is to raise floor for everyone.”

- `1:55-2:15` “Seb said real unlock is when everything connects on day one. That is core idea here too. All context in one place. Built-in tools, no setup. Finished work, not just answers.”

- `2:15-2:35` “I’ll show three workflows. Same pattern every time: context on left, AI coworker in middle, finished work on right.”

- `2:35-4:20` Demo 1. “First: existing-account whitespace analysis. Tinker pulls public signals, checks what account already owns from us, cross-references Keysight portfolio knowledge, and tells us what we should sell next. This is what Seb meant by ‘it just works because everything is already connected.’”

- `4:20-5:55` Demo 2. “Second: data enrichment. Here problem is not analysis, it is operational work. We start with raw company data. Tinker uses built-in enrichment flow and turns it into working pipeline data. Same story: connected tools in, usable output out.”

- `5:55-7:40` Demo 3. “Third: pre-meeting prep and follow-up. Tinker combines internal memory, meeting history, and profile context, then produces a prep brief and follow-up email. This is Seb’s memory point: AI gets useful when it remembers who you are, who you work with, and what you’re doing.”

- `7:40-8:20` “What matters is not any one demo. It is pattern underneath them. Once context, tools, and memory are connected, AI stops being generic chat and starts becoming real coworker.”

- `8:20-9:00` “Seb also said product itself is enablement. That is exactly point here. Most people will not get value from more AI training. They will get value from opening one tool, running one workflow, and immediately getting a result.”

- `9:00-9:35` “This is not about giving a few power users another tool. It is about giving other 90% of Keysight a way to actually work with state-of-the-art AI.”

- `9:35-10:00` “What excites me is what happens when floor rises for everyone at once. Sellers move faster. Ops moves faster. Meetings get smarter. More of Keysight can turn company context into finished work. We do not need to lower ceiling. We need to raise floor.”
